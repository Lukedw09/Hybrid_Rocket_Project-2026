# Launching a 20 kN·s-Class Rocket Near Tallahassee, Florida

**Purpose.** Referenced roadmap for certifications, licenses, site sizing, and practical options to launch a vehicle with about **20 kN·s** total impulse in or near Tallahassee, Florida.  
**Motor.** The project motor is **built by the team** (experimental / research hybrid, e.g. N₂O + paraffin)—**not** purchased as a commercial certified reload.  
**Builder age.** The primary builder is a **minor, age 17**. That changes who may buy oxidizer, who may be Flier of Record, and when Level 2/3 certification is possible.  
**Important.** Planning guidance, not legal advice. Confirm requirements with the FAA, Florida State Fire Marshal / local AHJ, landowner, Tripoli/NAR, and a parent/guardian before flying or hot-firing.

## 1. What “20 kN·s class” means

### 1.1 Conversion and impulse class

```text
  20 kN·s  =  20,000 N·s  ≈  4,496 lbf·s
```

| Scale | Value | Where 20 kN·s sits |
|-------|-------|---------------------|
| NAR/TRA letter class (impulse band) | **N-class equivalent** | N = 10,240.01–20,480.00 N·s |
| Next band up | O-class equivalent | 20,480.01–40,960.00 N·s |
| FAA Class 2 high-power limit | 40,960 N·s | **20 kN·s is Class 2** |
| FAA amateur-rocket ceiling | 889,600 N·s | 20 kN·s is well within this limit |

Because the motor is **homemade**, Tripoli treats it as a **Research Motor** (experimental), not a TMT/NAR-certified commercial motor—even if the impulse falls in the “N” band.

### 1.2 Regulatory bottom line (built hybrid)

A **20 kN·s built hybrid** flight is normally:

- An **FAA Class 2 — High-Power Rocket** under 14 CFR §101.22 (by impulse)  
- Still an **amateur rocket** under 14 CFR §1.1 (if altitude ≤ 150 km)  
- Operated under **14 CFR Part 101**, usually with an **FAA waiver/COA**  
- At Tripoli: a **Research Launch** item (research/hybrid motor rules)—**not** a normal “certified motor only” sport launch, and **not** usable for a Level 3 **certification** flight  
- **Not** allowed at NAR-sponsored launches (NAR does not permit experimental motors there)  
- Subject to **NFPA 1127** / Florida fire code and local AHJ rules  
- Subject to project N₂O/paraffin **fabrication and handling** safety rules  

### 1.3 Bottom line for a 17-year-old who is *building* the motor

**Building** (design, machine, cast paraffin, assemble hardware) is different from **being the legal/club-responsible flier**.

At **age 17** you generally **cannot**:

- Hold Tripoli/NAR **Level 2 or Level 3** (required to fly research/hybrid motors at Tripoli Research launches)  
- Be **Flier of Record** for a ~20 kN·s research hybrid at a TRA Research Launch  
- Purchase / take legal custody of **N₂O** cylinders and run fill/dump as the responsible person  
- Obtain a Florida **explosives** license/permit if one is ever required (**under 21** barred under §552.094)  

At **age 17** you **can**:

- **Design and fabricate** the hybrid motor and vehicle (grain, case, injector, airframe) under **adult supervision**  
- Lead analysis, drawings, test plans, and documentation  
- Join Tripoli Junior / NAR junior programs; earn **junior Level 1** skills on **commercial H–I** motors under a mentor (separate from this research motor—used to learn range discipline)  
- Participate in **adult-led** static tests and (if the Launch Director allows) support an **adult L2/L3 Flier of Record** on a research flight of *your* motor  

**Honest schedule for *you* to fly your built 20 kN·s hybrid as Flier of Record:**  
**18 → senior L1 → L2 (minimum for many research hybrid flights) → preferably L3 experience → Tripoli Research Launch.**

## 2. Built motor vs bought motor (club rules)

| Topic | Commercial certified motor | **Your built hybrid (this project)** |
|-------|----------------------------|--------------------------------------|
| Tripoli classification | Certified Motor | **Research Motor** / hybrid |
| Where it may fly (TRA) | Regular or Research launches (per rules) | **Tripoli Research Launch** (or BOD-approved equivalent)—not a normal cert-only sport launch |
| NAR launches | Allowed if certified | **Not allowed** (no EX motors) |
| Use for L3 certification flight | Required (single certified M/N/O) | **Forbidden**—homemade hybrid cannot certify L3 |
| Who may fly at Research Launch | Per certification level | Typically **Level 2 or 3** Tripoli member as flier |
| Age 17 as Flier of Record | No for N-class | **No** for research hybrid either |

Tripoli defines a **Hybrid Rocket Motor** as one where fuel and oxidizer are in different physical states (e.g., solid paraffin + liquid/vapor N₂O). **Research Motor** means non-certified, made for personal use, not sold for profit.

## 3. Age, parents, and adult roles

### 3.1 Why age 18 / L2 matter for a *built* hybrid

| Rule source | Effect on a 17-year-old motor builder |
|-------------|--------------------------------------|
| **Tripoli Research rules** | Research/hybrid motor flights and static tests are for members at the required certification level—commonly **L2 or L3**. Juniors do not hold L2/L3. |
| **Tripoli Mentoring Program** | Juniors **12–17** may learn HPR with a Senior mentor; mentor handles steps that legally require **18+** / certification. TMP does **not** authorize a junior to be Research Flier of Record on a 20 kN·s hybrid. |
| **NAR JrHPP** | Ages **14–17**; H–I commercial motors only under adult Flier of Record. **Does not** cover flying your experimental hybrid at NAR events. |
| **N₂O / oxidizer commerce** | Vendors and clubs expect an **adult** to purchase and control cylinders; remote fill/dump is an adult responsibility on the range. |
| **Florida §552.094** | No state explosives license/permit for natural persons **under 21** (if applicable to any energetics you use). |
| **CPSC/FHSA / HPR sales norms** | Relevant mainly to **commercial** HPR motor sales to 18+; your case is fabrication + oxidizer, which still needs adult legal custody of restricted materials. |

### 3.2 Required adults

| Role | Who | Duties for a *built* hybrid |
|------|-----|------------------------------|
| **Parent / legal guardian** | Required | Consents; waivers; landowner agreements; travel; medical; shop rules at home |
| **Technical mentor** | Experienced adult (ideally TRA L2/L3) | Reviews design; supervises machining/casting; range interface |
| **Flier of Record (flight or research static at TRA)** | TRA member **L2 or L3** (as required by Research rules), **18+** | Responsible flier; ensures Research Launch approval; controls pad/N₂O ops with LD/RSO |
| **N₂O custodian** | Adult | Buys, stores, transports, fills, dumps N₂O; owns cylinder accounts |
| **RSO / LD** | Club officials | Accept or reject the research motor/vehicle |

A parent who is **not** TRA L2/L3 **cannot** be Flier of Record for a Tripoli Research hybrid flight merely by being a parent.

### 3.3 What a 17-year-old motor builder should and should not do

**Do (fabrication & design)**

- Design the hybrid (grain, port, injector, case, seals, thermal protection)  
- Cast/machine paraffin grains under adult supervision and the project safety manual  
- Build airframe, recovery (prefer non-pyro while under junior HPR rules for *your* cert flights), avionics  
- Write test plans; reduce static-fire data; document every firing  
- Present the vehicle at RSO and answer technical questions  

**Do not (while under 18)**

- Be the person who **purchases or legally controls** N₂O for the project  
- Act as **responsible** fill/dump / pad officer for N₂O  
- Expect to fly the **research hybrid** as Flier of Record at a TRA Research Launch  
- Use the homemade hybrid as a **Level 3 certification** motor (forbidden at any age)  
- Hot-fire or fly without an adult safety lead and written SOP  
- Sign FAA/landowner documents as the sole adult responsible party  

### 3.5 How a parent becomes the mentor / Flier of Record

Tripoli allows **any Senior member** to act as a TMP **Mentor**. The mentor is the **Flyer of Record**, fully responsible for mentored flights, and may only use motors they can **legally purchase and possess** (and should stay within their **competence**, not only their card level).

**So a parent can be the mentor—if they join Tripoli as a Senior member and certify high enough for the work.**

#### Step-by-step (parent)

1. **Join Tripoli as a Senior member** (age 18+).  
   https://www.tripoli.org  

2. **Attend launches** at a Florida prefecture (Tampa, Space Coast, or Fort Myers) to learn range practice before certifying.

3. **Earn Level 1** (age 18+): build, fly, and recover a rocket on a **certified H or I** motor; witnessed by Prefect/TAP/Director.  
   - This is the minimum for mentoring junior **H–I** TMP / M1 flights.

4. **Earn Level 2** (after L1): pass the L2 exam, then fly a certified **J–L** motor.  
   - **Needed** for most **Tripoli Research** hybrid flights (research/hybrid fliers are typically L2 or L3).  
   - Without L2, the parent can mentor junior sport H–I flights but generally **cannot** be FoR for the built ~20 kN·s research hybrid at a Research Launch.

5. **Optional but useful: Level 3** (after L2): TAP process + certified **M/N/O** flight.  
   - Strengthens competence for large impulse; L3 cert flight still cannot use the homemade hybrid.

6. **Learn hybrid / N₂O operations** with experienced fliers before acting as FoR on the project motor (remote fill/dump, oxidizer cleanliness, Research Launch paperwork).

7. **On the range as mentor**  
   - Parent’s name and Tripoli number on the flight card as **Flyer of Record**  
   - Junior listed as rocket owner/builder as allowed  
   - Parent handles all HPR-certified materials and N₂O custody  
   - Parent stands with the junior at RSO  
   - Junior still needs **parent/guardian consent** on TMP paperwork (the mentoring parent can sign as guardian; if another adult is mentor, a parent must still consent)

8. **Junior side (your child)**  
   - Tripoli **Junior** membership  
   - Pass **TMP Training exam**; parent signs the certificate; email to `certifications@tripoli.org`  
   - Then TMP Level 1 (M1) flight with the parent-mentor on a **certified** H/I motor  

**Official TMP rules:** https://tripoli.org/content.aspx?club_id=795696&module_id=468986&page_id=22  

**Limits to be clear about**

| Goal | Parent needs |
|------|----------------|
| Mentor junior H–I / TMP M1 | Senior member + **at least Level 1** |
| FoR for **built ~20 kN·s research hybrid** | Senior member + typically **Level 2 or 3** + Research Launch approval + N₂O competence |
| Sign TMP consent only (not mentor) | Parent/guardian signature—**does not** require HPR certification |

Being the parent is **not** enough by itself. Being a **certified Tripoli Senior member** at the right level is what makes someone the mentor.

---

Building the motor concentrates risk in the **shop**, not the vendor counter:

- Paraffin melt/pour fires, burns, and **voids/cracks** that fail in flight (see safety manual)  
- Pressure-vessel work on cases and N₂O tanks—adult review of ratings and proof philosophy  
- Contamination of any hardware that will see N₂O (oils, organics)  
- Igniters / ejection energetics: adult custody if regulated  

Static-fire the built motor on a proper stand **before** any flight attempt.

## 4. Near Tallahassee: site reality

### 4.1 Local situation

- No Tripoli prefecture based in Tallahassee (Florida listings: Fort Myers, Space Coast, Tampa).  
- A ~20 kN·s research hybrid needs a **large** site (Section 7) plus a **Research Launch** framework—not a casual farm launch.  
- As a minor builder, plan on **parent + L2/L3 adult** for every propellant and flight operation.

### 4.2 Practical options

| Option | Notes | Fit for built 20 kN·s hybrid |
|--------|-------|------------------------------|
| **Tripoli Research Launch** in Florida (via Tampa / Space Coast / Fort Myers network or traveling research events) | Best match for EX hybrids | **Primary path** — adult L2/L3 Flier of Record while you are 17 |
| **Dedicated research event** on large private land | Landowner + AHJ + FAA waiver + TRA research rules | Possible later; heavy coordination |
| **University team** with faculty advisor | Insurance and adult FoR | Strong path for a student builder |
| NAR sport launch | EX motors not allowed | **Not** for this motor |
| Cecil Spaceport | Commercial spaceport | Overkill for this impulse |

**Practical advice:** Fabricate and static-test under adult supervision near Tallahassee if the shop is safe and legal; **fly** the research hybrid at an established **Tripoli Research** venue with an adult L2/L3 Flier of Record until you hold L2+ yourself.

    Build motor & vehicle (adult-supervised)

    Then YOU may be Flier of Record for your built hybrid at a Tripoli Research Launch

## 6. How to get the right certifications and licenses

### 6.1 While 17 — skills track (commercial H–I under mentor)

Use this **only** to learn range craft—not to qualify the research hybrid:

- **Tripoli Mentoring Program:** Junior 12–17; mentor handles age-restricted steps; junior L1 (M1) on certified H–I.  
- **NAR JrHPP:** Ages 14–17; adult Flier of Record for commercial HPR; parent consent; converts to senior L1 at 18.  

https://www.tripoli.org (TMP) · https://www.nar.org/JuniorHPRParticipationProgramProcedures

### 6.2 Research hybrid flight credentials (the project motor)

| When | Who flies your built hybrid at TRA Research |
|------|-----------------------------------------------|
| Age 17 | **Adult** Tripoli member meeting Research rules (**typically L2 or L3**) as Flier of Record |
| Age 18+ with L2 (or L3) | **You** may be Flier of Record for your research hybrid |

Confirm the current Tripoli Research Safety Code / Unified Safety Code with the Launch Director before every event (certification level, hybrid tank rules, remote fill/dump).

### 6.3 After 18 — recommended personal ladder

1. Convert junior L1 → senior L1 (keep membership current).  
2. Pass **Level 2** (exam + certified J–L flight).  
3. Optionally complete **Level 3** on a **commercial certified** M/N/O (separate rocket)—builds TAP discipline; **does not** use your hybrid.  
4. Submit your hybrid package to a Research Launch Director; fly as Flier of Record when approved.

### 6.4 FAA Part 101 Class 2 waiver

1. Site lat/long, max altitude, dates, propulsion (**N₂O + paraffin**, research hybrid).  
2. Club LD usually files the waiver/COA.  
3. Class 2 data generally **≥ 24 hours** before ops (§101.29).  
4. Stay inside waiver limits.

Minor note: waiver responsibility sits with the **operation** (LD / adult FoR), not with the 17-year-old builder alone.

### 6.5 Florida / local / ATF

- **NFPA 1127** via FAC 69A-3.012; notify **AHJ**.  
- **Ch. 552 / §552.094:** under-21 cannot hold state explosives licenses—adult handles any licensed explosives activity.  
- Landowner agreements: **parent** signs.  
- ATF: hobby APCP permit picture differs from hybrids; still expect adult control of regulated igniters/BP if used. Ask SFM/ATF for your exact bill of materials.

## 7. Launch site size (NFPA / NAR distance tables)

For installed impulse in the **N** band (10,240–20,480 N·s):

| Min. cleared diameter | Min. personnel distance | Complex rocket personnel distance |
|----------------------|-------------------------|-----------------------------------|
| 125 ft | **1,000 ft** | **1,500 ft** |

Site minimum dimension = greater of: **½ max expected/waived altitude**, **1,500 ft**, or **twice** personnel distance (~2,000–3,000 ft).

High altitude often dominates (e.g., 10,000 ft AGL → ~5,000 ft site dimension).

Launcher typically **≥ 1,500 ft** from occupied buildings and busy highways.

## 9. Step-by-step roadmap (17-year-old builder of the hybrid)

### Phase A — Now (age 17): build and prove on the ground

1. Parent + technical mentor + (for any TRA flight) L2/L3 adult FoR identified.  
2. Join Tripoli Junior; complete TMP; optional NAR JrHPP for H–I skills.  
3. Fabricate subscale hybrids first; static-fire with adult N₂O control.  
4. Scale toward 20 kN·s on the stand; document performance.  
5. Build flight vehicle; do not skip static proof.

### Phase B — Optional flight while 17 (adult FoR)

1. Tripoli **Research Launch** only.  
2. Adult **L2/L3** Flier of Record; adult N₂O custody.  
3. You: builder/presenter at RSO; work within LD limits.  
4. Parent on-site; all waivers signed.  

This is **not** your certification flight.

### Phase C — After 18: you become Flier of Record

1. L1 → **L2** (required for typical research hybrid flier status).  
2. Optional L3 on a **commercial** motor.  
3. Fly **your** built ~20 kN·s hybrid at a Research Launch as FoR.

### Phase D — Site

Default: established Florida Research-capable prefecture/event.  
Local Tallahassee field: only with full distance, AHJ, waiver, and adult structure.

## 11. Airspace authorization reminder

FAA airspace authorization is still required when §101.25 limits are exceeded—typical for high N-class-equivalent altitudes. A commercial spaceport slot is not required for a normal Part 101 Class 2 research hybrid flight.
