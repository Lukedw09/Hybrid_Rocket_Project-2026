## Equations captured

Thin-wall hoop and longitudinal stress:

```text
  sigma_H  =  P * d / (2 * t)
  sigma_L  =  P * d / (4 * t)
```

Bolt-hole tear-out (conservative; shear area from hole edge to case end):

```text
  tau_shear  =  P * (pi * (d/2)^2)  /  ( N_bolts * 2 * A_shear )

  A_shear  =  t * (edge distance from hole to case end)
```

Practical spacing: ≥ **1.0–2.0×** bolt diameter from edge and between bolts (AISC J3.4 cited).

## Phoenix example (wiki)

- P = 826 psi, d = 4 in, N = 24 bolts, wall t = 0.25 in  
- Averaged edge distances → A_shear ≈ 0.28 in²  
- Resulting tear-out shear ≈ 772 psi (well below Al shear capacity)

## Relevance to this project

Confirms that for **radially bolted** aluminum cases, **tear-out and ligament tension between holes** often govern before bare-barrel hoop stress — especially on thin walls. Supports analyzing closures separately from Sutton hoop sizing.
