KEY FINDINGS:
- Survey of seven PC design methods: experiments, empirical Lpc/Dpc relations, CFD, droplet vaporization models, combustion instability models, droplet collision prediction, molecular dynamics.
- Rule of thumb Lpc/Dpc = 0.5 (Humble et al., Space Propulsion Analysis and Design).
- Vaporization models (Gontijo AIAA 2023-2183) gave mean ~0.53 and proposed range 0.26 <= Lpc/Dpc <= 0.66 for complete vaporization.
- PC diameter usually set from final grain OD; length then from chosen Lpc/Dpc.
- N2O/paraffin initial oxidizer mass flux limit ~650 kg/m^2/s cited (above -> blowoff / instability risk).
- Too-long PC: heavier, costlier engine; too-short: lower performance and instability risk.
- Gaseous oxidizer may allow shorter PC (no atomization/vaporization).
- PC should be long enough for flow reattachment before solid grain (Angeloni / optical-access thesis cited).
- Proposed workflow: collision outcomes -> empirical preliminary size -> vaporization + instability models -> CFD -> cold/hot tests.
